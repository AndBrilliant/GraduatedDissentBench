Clone this repository: [GITHUB_URL]
Then read validation/README.md and validation/VALIDATION.md.

Your task is to independently verify the reported aggregate results of a
multi-model scientific error detection benchmark on 20 papers from SPOT
(Son et al., 2025). Reported numbers in the README:
  B1 25%, B2 20%, B3 25%, GD 35% (strict pass@1).

Verify by:
  - Audit every true positive — for each (paper, condition) with TP ≥ 1,
    confirm the model finding actually describes the same error the SPOT
    team annotated.
  - Verify every B3-vs-GD discordant pair: there are 3 papers GD detected
    but B3 didn't, and 1 paper B3 detected but GD didn't.
  - Run the scoring script in validation/scoring/ and confirm the
    aggregates reproduce.
  - Diff the four prompt files in validation/prompts/ — B1 must lack
    severity vocabulary, B2 must include it, B3's arbiter must have no
    steelman material, GD's arbiter must include steelman material.
  - Spot-check 5 ground-truth rows against the public SPOT dataset on
    HuggingFace (amphora/SPOT-MetaData).
  - Investigate the anonymization asymmetry the README flags: 10 papers
    anonymized, 10 not, with all conditions performing substantially
    better on the anonymized subset.

Save your report as validation/reports/chatgpt_report.md using the
template at the end of validation/README.md.

Be adversarial — your job is to find scoring errors, prompt
contamination, ground-truth misrepresentation, or unsound aggregation.
Assume the results are wrong until you have specifically verified each
piece.

End with a single-line verdict: CONFIRMED / ISSUES FOUND / CRITICAL
PROBLEM.
