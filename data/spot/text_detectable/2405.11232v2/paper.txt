Abstract. In this paper, we study multiplication formula of \( F \)-polynomial of representations of Hernandez and Leclerc’s quivers with potentials. Since the truncated \( q \)-characters of some real simple modules over a quantum affine group \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) can be expressed in terms of such \( F \)-polynomials, one can describe the product of two simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) using this multiplication formula.

Consider a simple Lie algebra \( \mathfrak{g} \). The quantum affine algebra for \( \mathfrak{g} \), denoted as \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \), holds significant importance across various disciplines. Central to understanding representations of \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) is the concept of \( q \)-characters, introduced by Frenkel and Reshetikhin [FR98]. However, detailing the \( q \)-characters of all simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) proves intricate. To address this complexity, Hernandez and Leclerc introduced the truncated \( q \)-character for simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) [HL10]. Subsequently, they expressed these truncated \( q \)-characters of Kirillov–Reshetikhin modules in terms of \( F \)-polynomials of modules over their quivers \( Q_{HL} \) with potentials [HL16]. Extending this to a broader class of simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \), Kashiwara, Kim, Oh, and Park demonstrated that the truncated \( q \)-characters of real simple modules corresponding to cluster monomials can be described using \( F \)-polynomials of representations of \( Q_{HL} \) with potentials [KKOP24].

Consequently, understanding the product of real simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \) necessitates examining the product of \( F \)-polynomials of two corresponding representations of \( Q_{HL} \) with potentials.

On a separate note, the multiplication formula has garnered attention from numerous experts [CK08, GLS07, Pal12, XX09, KPQ23]. However, much of the focus lies on triangular categories or 2-Calabi–Yau categories. In this study, we delve into the category of representations of \( Q_{HL} \) with potentials. It’s worth mentioning that \( Q_{HL} \) can be identified with the Gabriel quiver of an endomorphism ring of a tilting object \( T \) of the Frobenius category of modules over a preprojective algebra. However, whether this endomorphism ring is the Jacobian algebra for \( Q_{HL} \) remains unknown, rendering our findings novel.

As a result, we establish a connection between the head of the product of two real simple modules and the generic extension of modules over the Jacobian algebra for \( Q_{HL} \), akin to [LM21, Conjecture 5.1].

= \sum_Y \big( \chi(\mathbb{P}E(M_1, M_2)_Y) + \chi(\mathbb{P}E(M_2, M_1)_Y ) \big) \chi(\mathrm{Gr}_\beta(Y))
\tag{3.16}
\]

In particular, when \(\mathrm{Ext}^1_A(M_1, M_2) = 1\), we obtain

\[
F_{M_1} \cdot F_{M_2} = F_{Y_1} + F_{Y_2}
\tag{3.17}
\]

where \(Y_1\) subjects to \(0 \to M_1 \to Y_1 \to M_2 \to 0\) and \(Y_2\) subjects to \(0 \to M_2 \to Y_2 \to M_1 \to 0\).

Let \(\mathfrak{g}\) be the simple Lie algebra over \(\mathbb{C}\). We denote by \(\alpha_i\) (\(i \in I\)) the simple roots of \(\mathfrak{g}\), and by \(\omega_i\) the fundamental weights of \(\mathfrak{g}\). Let \(\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\) be the corresponding untwisted affine Lie algebra and \(U_q(\hat{\mathfrak{g}})\) be the Drinfeld-Jimbo quantum enveloping algebra of \(\hat{\mathfrak{g}}\), see e.g. [CP95].

Frenkel and Reshetikhin [FR98] have attached to every complex finite-dimensional representation \(M\) of \(U_q(\hat{\mathfrak{g}})\) a q-character \(\chi_q(M)\). By definition, the q-character \(\chi_q(M)\) is a Laurent polynomial with positive integer coefficients in the infinite set of variables

\[
Y = \{Y_{i,a} \mid i \in I, a \in \mathbb{C}^* \},
\]

which should be seen as a quantum affine analogue of \(\{ e^{\omega_i} \mid i \in I \}\). In this paper, we will be concerned only with polynomials involving the subset of variables

\[
Y_{i, q^r}, \; ((i, r) \in W).
\]

For simplicity of notation, we shall therefore write \(Y_{i,r}\) instead of \(Y_{i,q^r}\).

Let \(m\) be a dominant monomial in the variables \(Y_{i,r} \in Y\), that is, a monomial with nonnegative exponents. We denote by \(L(m)\) the corresponding irreducible representation of \(U_q(\hat{\mathfrak{g}})\), and by \(\chi_q(m) = \chi_q(L(m))\) its q-character. For example, if \(m\) is of the form

\[
m = \prod_{j=0}^{k-1} Y_{i, r+j b_i}, \qquad (i \in I, r \in \mathbb{Z}, k \geq 1)
\tag{4.1}
\]

\(L(m)\) is called Kirillov-Reshetikhin module, and usually denoted by \(W^{(i)}_{k,r}\). \(\mathcal{C}^-\) denotes the monomial subcategory of modules generated by fundamental module \(V_{i,a}\) for all \((i, a) \in \Gamma^-\).

Finally, following [FR98], for $(i, r) \in V$ we introduce the following quantum affine analogue of $e^{\alpha_i}$:

\[
A_{i,r} := Y_{i, r-d_i} Y_{i, r+d_i}
\left( \prod_{j : c_{ji} = -1} Y_{j,r} \right)^{-1}
\left( \prod_{j : c_{ji} = -2} Y_{j, r-1} Y_{j, r+1} \right)^{-1}
\left( \prod_{j : c_{ji} = -3} Y_{j, r-2} Y_{j, r} Y_{j, r+2} \right)^{-1}
\qquad (4.2)
\]

To relate the $q$-character of simple modules with the $F$-polynomials for the algebra $A$, we need more notations.

Let
\[
Y^- = \{ Y_{i,r} \in Y \mid (i, r) \in W^- \}.
\]

For $(i, r) \in W^-$, we perform the substitution
\[
z_{i,r} = \prod_{k \geq 0, \, r + k b_{ii} \leq 0} Y_{i, r + k b_{ii}}
\]

In the sequel, we shall evaluate the variables of the $F$-polynomials as the inverses of the variables $A_{i,r}$ introduced in (4.2), namely:
\[
v_{i, r} = A_{i, r}^{-1} \qquad (4.3)
\]

Our $F$-polynomials are given by the equation (3.1).

Next, we will introduce a class of modules over $A$: Let $I_{i, m}$ be the (infinite-dimensional) injective $A$-module with socle isomorphic to $S_{i,m}$. The $\mathbb{C}$-vector space $I_{i, m}$ has a basis indexed by classes modulo $J$ of paths in $\Gamma^-$ with final vertex $(i, m)$. Let $K_{k, m}^{(i)}$ be the kernel of a generic $A$-module homomorphism from $I_{i, m}$ to $I_{i, m - k b_{ii}}$.

One also defines such modules on generalized preprojective algebra $\tilde{\Pi}$. For each $i \in I$ and $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{>0}$, we define the graded $\tilde{\Pi}$-module $K_k^{(i)}$ by
\[
K_k^{(i)} := q^{k d_i} \mathbb{D} \left( (\tilde{\Pi} / \tilde{\Pi} \epsilon_i^k) e_i \right) \qquad (4.4)
\]

From the definition, the module $K_k^{(i)}$ fits into the following short exact sequence:
\[
0 \to K_k^{(i)} \to q^{k d_i} I_i(\infty) \xrightarrow{\epsilon_i^k} q^{-k d_i} I_i(\infty) \to 0. \qquad (4.5)
\]

Following [FM23, Remark 5.2], we obtain
\[
\Phi \left( q^{r - k d_i} K_k^{(i)} \right) = K_{k, r}^{(i)}
\]

In [HL16], we have the following theorem.

Theorem 4.1. [HL16, Theorem 4.8] Let $(i, r) \in V^-$ and $k \in \mathbb{N}$. We have
\[
\chi_q^-\left( W_{k, r - (2k-1)d_i}^{(i)} \right) = \left( \prod_{s=1}^k Y_{i, r - (2s-1) d_i} \right) F_{K_{i, r}^{(i)}}
\]

where the variable \( v_{i,r} \) of the F-polynomial are evaluated as in (4.3).

In general, Hernandez and Leclerc [HL16] showed that

**Theorem 4.2.** [HL16, Theorem 5.1; Conjecture 5.2] The cluster algebra \( \mathcal{A} \) is identified with the image of the injective ring homomorphism from \( K_0(C^-) \) to \( \mathbb{Z}[Y_{i,r} \mid (i,r) \in Y^-] \) given by \( L(m) \mapsto \chi_q^-(m) \). Moreover, the cluster monomials get identified with the truncated \( q \)-characters of real simple modules.

Note that the last statement was proved by Kashiwara, Kim, Oh and Park in [KKOP24].

**Proposition 4.3.** Let \( m_1, m_2 \) be two cluster monomials in \( \mathcal{A} \), \( L_1, L_2 \) be corresponding simple modules over \( U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{g}}) \), and \( M_1, M_2 \) be the corresponding rigid modules over \( \mathcal{A} \). The tensor product \( L_1 \otimes L_2 \) is simple if and only if

\[
\mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_1, M_2) = 0
\]

*Proof.* If \( L_1 \otimes L_2 \) is a simple module, then there exists a rigid module \( T \) over \( \mathcal{A} \) such that

\[
F_{M_1} \cdot F_{M_2} = F_T \text{ and } g_T = g_{M_1} + g_{M_2}
\]

where \( g_M \) is the g-vector for module \( M \). It implies that there exists a short exact sequence

\[
0 \to M_1 \to T \to M_2 \to 0 \text{ or } 0 \to M_2 \to T \to M_1 \to 0
\]

If \( \mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_1, M_2) \neq 0 \), then by theorem 3.2, we get

\[
\chi(\mathbb{P}\mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_1, M_2)) F_T = \sum_Y ( \chi(\mathbb{P}\mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_1, M_2)_Y ) + \chi(\mathbb{P}\mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_2, M_1)_Y ) ) F_Y
\]

since \( T \) appears in the right hand of the above equation, we have \( Y = T \) for all \( Y \) in the right hand of the above equation, which implies that

\[
F_T = 2F_T
\]

It is a contradiction. Hence, we prove our claim.

If \( \mathrm{Ext}_{\mathcal{A}}^1(M_1, M_2) = 0 \), then \( M_1 \oplus M_2 \) is a rigid module. The truncated \( q \)-character of \( L_1 \otimes L_2 \) is equal to

\[
z^{g_1+g_2} F_{M_1 \oplus M_2}
\]

which is a cluster monomial. It means that \( L_1 \otimes L_2 \) is a simple module. □
