Abstract. In this paper, we first present an explicit expression for the inverse of a type of matrices. As special applications, the inverse of some matrices arising from implicit time integration techniques, such as the well-known implicit Runge-Kutta schemes and block implicit methods, can also be explicitly determined. Additionally, we introduce three fast algorithms for computing the elements of the inverse of these matrices in O(n²) arithmetic operations, i.e., the first one is based on Traub algorithm for fast inversion of Vandermonde matrices, while the other two utilize the special structure of the matrices. Finally, some symbolic and numerical results are presented to show that our algorithms are both highly efficient and accurate.

Key words. Matrix inversion, fast algorithm, Runge-Kutta matrix, implicit time integration

1. Introduction. General speaking, it is unnecessary and inadvisable to actually compute matrix inverse in the vast majority of practical computational problems. However, there exist some matrices and their inverse play a very important role in practical applications. In this paper, we investigate a type of matrices with special structure and design some fast algorithms to compute their inverse. The implicit Runge-Kutta (IRK) methods and block implicit methods (BIM) have been widely used for the time discretization of ordinary and partial differential equations [6, 4, 10, 17, 16, 18, 21, 22]. The inverse of IRK matrix and BIM matrix are necessary for stability analysis, and plays an important role in the construction of new time discretization methods with desirable properties [2, 17]. In this paper, we

prove that the inverse of the IRK matrix and BIM matrix is the submatrix of \( W^T \),
i.e., the inverse of IRK matrix and BIM matrix is exactly \( W_n^T \) if \( c_i \) (\( 1 \leq i \leq n \)) are
the zeros of the Legendre polynomials and \( c_i = i \) (\( 1 \leq i \leq n \)), respectively. Consider
a parabolic equation of the form

(1.2) \[ u_t - \nabla \cdot (\alpha(x)\nabla u) + \beta(x) \cdot \nabla u = f(x, t), \quad \text{in}~\Omega \times (0,T] \]

equipped with appropriate initial and boundary conditions, where \( \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^d \) (\( d = 2 \) or 3) is a bounded, open polygonal (or polyhedral) domain, \( f(x, t) \in L^2(\Omega \times [0, T]) \),
\( 0 < \alpha_0 \leq \alpha(x) \leq \alpha_1 < +\infty \), and all the coefficients are sufficiently smooth. The
discretization of (1.2) by IRK in time and finite element in space produces a system
of equations with the coefficient matrix

(1.3) \[ I \otimes M + \tau A \otimes K, \]

where \( \otimes \) denotes the Kronecker product, \( \tau \) is the time step, \( I \in \mathbb{R}^{r \times r} \), \( A \in \mathbb{R}^{r \times r} \),
\( M \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times N} \) and \( K \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times N} \) denote identity matrix, the coefficient matrix of \( r \)-
stage IRK, the mass matrix and the stiffness matrix, respectively. In practice, (1.3)
is often large and highly ill-conditioned, and parallel preconditioners are necessary to
construct for solving the system with coefficient matrix (1.3). Usually, it is preferable
to solve an equivalent system with the coefficient matrix

(1.4) \[ A^{-1} \otimes M + \tau I \otimes K \]

since it can significantly reduce the computational cost [1, 3, 5, 10, 19]. Moreover, it
requires less memory since \( A^{-1} \otimes M \) is in general sparser than \( A \otimes K \), and the stiff
part of the matrix is block diagonal so that the analysis and solution more tractable
[24]. Various preconditioners based on \( A^{-1} \) are constructed, such as, ILU(0)-based
stage-coupled and stage-parallel preconditioners [20], linear and nonlinear parallel
preconditioner [25, 24], and the lower triangular preconditioners [1]. Hence, the
computation of \( A^{-1} \) is very important for solving the system of equations arising from
the discretization of time-dependent PDEs, and we will present an explicit formula to
compute \( A^{-1} \).

The explicit formula for the elements of \( W \) is well suited for hand or machine
calculation. However, the computation of the \( (n + 1)^2 \) elements requires \( O(n^3) \) arithmetic
operations, and it is clearly too expensive in compute time for large \( n \). In
this paper, we present three fast algorithms to compute all the elements of \( W \) in
only \( O(n^2) \) floating point operations (flops). The explicit formula for the Vandermonde
matrix was first presented by Traub in [26], and an algorithm with the complexity
\( 6n^2 \) flops was also proposed by using its special structure. This algorithm has been
generalized to invert the Vandermonde-like matrices [7, 8, 9, 12, 23, 27]. Following
the idea in [26], we first present a fast algorithm to compute all the elements of \( W \)
in only \( O(n^2) \) flops. Similar to Traub algorithm for Vandermonde matrix, this algorithm is numerically unstable and inaccurate due to the rapid propagation of roundoff
errors. Moreover, the solution components may soon exceed the representable machine number and produce overflow as \( n \) increases. Actually, we observe that \( W \) has 
better structure than Vandermonde matrix although it is singular. By using a trick
to avoid overflow and underflow in multiplication and division, we derive another two
algorithms with \( O(n^2) \) complexity. It is shown that our algorithms are both highly
efficient and accurate for computing all the elements of \( W \).

## 2.2. The explicit formula for the inverse of IRK matrix and BIM matrix

The IRK methods based on Gaussian and Radau quadrature formulae possess favorable stability properties, offer a high order of accuracy, and have no start-up issues. In this section, we present the explicit formula for the coefficient matrix of Gauss and Radau IIA methods. The elements of these matrices are given by

\[
(2.14) \qquad a_{ij} = \int_0^{c_i} l_j(\theta) d\theta,
\]

where \( l_i(\theta) \) is the Lagrange polynomial \( \prod_{k\neq i}(\theta - c_k)/(c_i - c_k) \) and \( c_i \) \((1 \leq i \leq n)\) are the zeros of

\[
\frac{d^n}{dx^n}\left(x^n(x-1)^n\right), \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{d^n}{dx^{n-1}}\left(x^{n-1}(x-1)^n\right),
\]

respectively. Due to \( \theta^{k-1} = \sum_{j=1}^n c_j^{k-1} l_j(\theta) \) for \( k = 1,2,\ldots,n \), (2.14) is equivalent to the following systems [11]

\[
(2.15) \qquad \sum_{j=1}^n a_{ij} c_j^{k-1} = \frac{c_i^k}{k}, \quad k = 1,2,\ldots,n, \quad i = 1,2,\ldots,n.
\]

From (2.15), we have

(2.16) \qquad A = C_n V_n D_n^{-1} V_n^{-1},

\[

3. The fast algorithms for the inverse of the matrix. The explicit formula for \( W \) is suitable for symbolic operations using a symbolic manipulation package. However, it may be very expensive for large \( n \). In this section, we present three fast numerical algorithms with \( O(n^2) \) complexity to compute \( W \).

Following the idea of Traub' algorithm for the inverse of Vandermonde matrix \( V \) [26], we introduce the first algorithm to compute the elements of \( W \). Firstly, we calculate the coefficients \( a_k \) of the monic polynomial \( \varphi(x) \) in (2.2) recursively by

\[
\left\{
\begin{aligned}
&\beta_{m+1, m+1} = 1, \quad \beta_{m+1, i} = \beta_{m, i-1} - c_m \beta_{m, i}, \\
& a_k = \beta_{n+1, k},
\end{aligned}
\right.
\]
for \( 1 \leq m \leq n, \; 0 \leq i \leq m, \; 0 \leq k \leq n+1, \)

where \( \beta_{1,0} = -c_0, \; \beta_{1,1} = 1 \) and \( \beta_{m,-1} = 0 \). Then, we calculate the coefficients \( b_k \) of \( \psi(x) \) in (2.5) by
\[
b_k = a_{k+1} + c_i b_{k+1}, \quad n \geq k \geq 0,
\]
where \( b_{n+1} = 0 \). Finally, we calculate the scalars \( \psi(c_i) \) by Horner's rule and obtain
\[
(3.1) \quad
W_{ij} =
\begin{cases}
\frac{1}{c_j - c_i} \frac{\psi(c_j)}{\psi(c_i)}, & i \neq j, \\[2ex]
\displaystyle\sum_{k=0, k \neq i}^{n} \frac{1}{c_i - c_k}, & i = j,
\end{cases}
\]

The above scheme is implemented thoroughly in Algorithm 3.1. We see that the computational complexity of Algorithm 3.1 is \( 11n^2 + 12n \) flops. However, it may soon exceed the representable machine number and produce overflow (or underflow) when computing the coefficients \( a_k \) and \( b_k \) for large \( n \), this issue also occurs in computing \( \psi(c_i) \) since it involving a product of \( n \) factors.

In order to avoid the above issue and improve accuracy, we present another fast algorithm by using the special structure of \( W \). Firstly, we introduce the matrix \( G \) which is given by
\[
(3.2) \quad
G = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & c_0 - c_1 & c_0 - c_2 & \cdots & c_0 - c_{n-1} & c_0 - c_n \\
c_1 - c_0 & 1 & c_1 - c_2 & \cdots & c_1 - c_{n-1} & c_1 - c_n \\
c_2 - c_0 & c_2 - c_1 & 1 & \cdots & c_2 - c_{n-1} & c_2 - c_n \\
\vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots \\
c_{n-1} - c_0 & c_{n-1} - c_1 & c_{n-1} - c_2 & \cdots & 1 & c_{n-1} - c_n \\
c_n - c_0 & c_n - c_1 & c_n - c_2 & \cdots & c_n - c_{n-1} & 1
\end{pmatrix}
\]

We first compute the first row of \( \widetilde{W} \) by

## 4. Numerical experiments.

**Example 2.** (Radau IIA nodes in (0,1]): $c_0 = 0$ and $c_i$ ($i=1,2,\cdots,n$) are the zeros of $\frac{d^n}{dx^{n-1}}(x^{n-1}(x-1)^n)$.

5. Conclusion. We proposed an explicit formula to compute the inverse of a type of matrices which include the well-known fully IRK and BIM matrices as special cases. So the inverse of these matrices arising from implicit time integration can be computed by the basic four arithmetic operations of the quadrature nodes, and we can obtain their inverse exactly by using a symbolic manipulation package. It’s shown that the inverse of these matrices is much easier to compute than themselves.

by Mathematica. We also presented three fast algorithms with \(O(n^2)\) complexity to compute the inverse of these matrices. The numerical results demonstrate that Algorithm 3.3 is very fast and it only requires several seconds to obtain the inverse of the matrices with size \(n = 10000\). Although encountering substantial cancellation in the computation of diagonal elements with Radau IIA nodes and Chebyshev nodes, we can also obtain desirable high accuracy by using higher precision, such as quadruple precision, and the compute time is considerably less than that required by using the symbolic manipulation package.
