Paper: R03
Title: High replicability of newly discovered social-behavioural findings is achievable
Journal: Nature Human Behaviour (2024)
Retraction: September 2024

Retraction cause: Misstatement of preregistered hypotheses and post-hoc analysis presented as confirmatory.

Specific errors (for scoring):
- Claimed preregistration of hypotheses and measures that were not actually preregistered
- Selection of outcome measures and analyses with knowledge of the data (post-hoc presented as confirmatory)
- Inconsistent definition of "original discoveries" reference set across different calculations
- Incomplete reporting of data and analyses

Text-detectable signals:
- Circular reasoning in power calculations (using observed effect sizes to justify replication rates)
- Selection bias in discovery process (labs submitted only discoveries they "believed" had effects)
- Discrepancy between claimed preregistration and actual methodology
- Post-hoc power claims (96% average power calculated after observing results)
- Missing information about how many pilot studies were conducted but not submitted
