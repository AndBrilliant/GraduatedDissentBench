Paper: R01
Title: Post-COVID-19 Condition in Children
Journal: JAMA Pediatrics (2023)
Retraction: August 2024

Retraction cause: Multiple coding errors in data processing led to substantial underestimation of post-COVID condition incidence in children.

Specific errors (for scoring):
- Children with missing symptom data were miscoded as asymptomatic instead of being excluded from analysis
- 15 children were wrongly excluded due to incorrect date cutoff logic
- Study included participants outside the stated 8-13 age range (ages 1-19 were actually included) without disclosure
- Incorrect symptom association coding affected Cox regression results
- Reported PCC incidence of 0.4% was actually 1.4% — a >3x underestimate

Text-detectable signals:
- Hazard ratios reported as 0.00 with extremely narrow CIs (0.00-0.02, 0.00-0.09) — statistically impossible, indicates model convergence failure
- Only 1 out of 271 children met PCC criteria — inadequate for Cox regression
- Sample size insufficient for the number of covariates in the Cox model
- Reported 0.4% incidence is substantially lower than comparable published estimates
