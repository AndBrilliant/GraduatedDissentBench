Paper: R10
Title: Vitamin D and COVID-19 morbidity
Journal: PLOS ONE (2022)
Retraction: September 2025

Retraction cause: Fatal design flaw — selection bias from including only patients who already had vitamin D testing, combined with implausible data distributions.

Specific errors (for scoring):
- Only included patients who had already undergone vitamin D testing — introduces severe selection bias
- Delay between vitamin D measurement and COVID-19 infection ranged 14-730 days — levels may have changed substantially
- Cannot assess association between pre-infection vitamin D and disease severity with this design

Text-detectable signals:
- Virtually all deceased patients had vitamin D below normal ranges (implausible uniformity)
- Complete overlap between patients with COPD and hypertension (bizarre comorbidity pattern)
- 15 patients had exactly 10 ng/mL vitamin D; 10 of those died — suspicious clustering
- Selection on the dependent variable visible in methods (only tested patients included)
