Paper: R25
Title: Wildfire Smoke Exposure and Incident Dementia
Journal: JAMA Neurology (2024)
Retraction: June 2025 (retracted and replaced)

Retraction cause: Coding error in dementia outcome definition — used OR instead of AND for diagnostic criteria, adding thousands of false cases.

Specific errors (for scoring):
- ICD code logic used OR instead of AND for Parkinson disease + secondary dementia diagnosis
- This added 5,413 individuals without actual dementia to the case group
- Incorrectly excluded 5,345 KPSC members with Parkinson disease
- Primary finding changed from significant (OR 1.18, CI 1.03-1.34) to nonsignificant (OR 1.12, CI 0.98-1.28)

Text-detectable signals:
- The ICD coding logic may be visible in the methods section (OR vs AND condition)
- The primary result OR 1.18 is barely significant (CI just above 1.00) — fragile finding
- Large cohort study (1.2M) but primary effect is very small — sensitivity to case definition is high
- Note: Class B detectability — the exact coding error requires knowing the ICD logic, but the fragility of the result should raise concern
